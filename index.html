RocBoi Quez Prepares to Release Get Out Your Feelings (Chopped Not Slopped)

Underground Atlanta Hip-Hop Meets Raw Emotion | February 20th via Bentley Records LLC

Atlanta’s underground hip-hop scene has long been a proving ground for artists who live through their music, and RocBoi Quez stands firmly in that tradition. On February 20th, RocBoi Quez is set to release Get Out Your Feelings (Chopped Not Slopped) under Bentley Records LLC—a slowed, reimagined extension of his original, emotionally charged record.

The original version of Get Out Your Feelings carried a gritty, unfiltered energy that reflected real-life struggles rather than polished industry narratives. RocBoi Quez has never shied away from telling his truth, openly stating, After loosing my mother, I felt numb. Suffered from PTSD, fought a battle of being lost. That pain is embedded in every verse, giving the record its unmistakable weight and authenticity.

The chopped not slopped version expands the song’s versatility while preserving its emotional core. Slowed tempos, stretched melodies, and deeper emphasis on vocal delivery allow listeners to fully absorb the meaning behind the lyrics. Where the original pushes forward with urgency, the chopped version invites reflection—turning the track into a cinematic experience rooted in Southern hip-hop culture.

This release further solidifies RocBoi Quez’s position within Atlanta’s underground movement while bridging regional sounds, connecting the city’s raw street energy with the hypnotic influence of chopped culture. Fans and industry insiders alike have begun to anticipate what’s next.

Looking ahead, RocBoi Quez is also preparing for an anticipated VipAccessPublishing release in 2026, signaling continued growth and broader distribution while maintaining his independent spirit. Get Out Your Feelings (Chopped Not Slopped) is not just a remix—it’s a statement of resilience, evolution, and artistic survival.
